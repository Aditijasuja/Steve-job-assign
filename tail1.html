The Real Leadership Lessons of Steve Jobs

Six Months after jobs's death, the author of the best selling biography identifies the practices that every CEO can try to emulate.by Walter Isaacson

His sage is the entrepreneual creation myth writ large: Steve jobs cofounded the apple in his parents garage in 1976, was outside in 1985, returned to rescue it from the bankcrupty in 1997 and by the time he died in Octobar 2011, had built into the most valuable company. Along the way he helped to transform the seven industries: Personal computing, animated movies, tablets, Music, Phone, tablet computing, retail stores and digital publishing. He thus belong in the pantheon of Americas's great innovators, along with Thomas Edison, Henry ford, and Walt Disney. None of these men was a saint, but along there after their personalities are forgotten, History will remember how they applied to imagination to technology and business.

"The people who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do."
-Apple's "Think's Different" commericail 1997

One of the last time i saw him, after i had finishing writing most of the book. I asked him again about his tendency to be rough on people. "Look at the results," he replied. "These are all smart people i work with, and any of them could get a top job at another place if they were truly feeling brutalized. But they don't." Then he paused for a few minutes and said,almost wisefully. "And we got amaxing thigs done. "Indeed, he and apple had a string of hits over the past dozen years that was greater than that of any other innovative company in modern times. iMac,ipad,App Store, OS X Lion -not to mention every pixar film. And as he battled his final illness. Jobs was surrounded by an intensely loyal cadre of collegues who had been inspired by him for years and a very loving wife, sister and 4 children.
